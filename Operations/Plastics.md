# Plastics.md — Salvaged Polymers & Pyrolytic Fuel Recovery

> ⚠️ **Operational Safety Advisory**
> Thermal depolymerization (pyrolysis) operates at high temperatures (300°C–500°C) and generates highly flammable, toxic hydrocarbons and synthetic gas. Enclosure breach or oxygen ingress creates an immediate risk of explosive pressure buildup or flashback fire. Run-off gases contain chlorine compounds (if PVC is present) and other hazardous airborne byproducts. Air Scrubber operation, continuous venting, and oxygen exclusion are non-negotiable prerequisites before heating begins. See Operations/Air_Scrubber.md.

## File Purpose

This document establishes the processing path for salvaged plastics within the Lazarus Forge. While mechanical repurposing (such as drawing custom 3D printing filament or RepRap stock) is prioritized for high-purity single-source polymers, mixed or degraded consumer plastic bulk represents a significant logistical hazard. This file details the alternative recovery method: converting low-grade plastic waste into usable thermal or mechanical energy via pyrolytic fuel recovery.

## Body: Polymer Processing & Fuel Recovery Doctrine

### I. General Plastics Triage Hierarchy
Consistent with the principle of *Salvage Before Reduction*, plastics enter a progressive depth triage sequence:
1. **Direct Component Reuse:** Structural panels, enclosures, or functional hardware are preserved intact.
2. **Mechanical Repurposing (RepRap / Filament):** High-purity, cleanly identified single-stream plastics (e.g., clean PLA, ABS, PETG) are routed toward shredding and drawing into custom fabrication stock. 
3. **Thermal Depolymerization (Pyrolysis):** Mixed, degraded, contaminated, or low-value bulk plastics bypass mechanical extrusion and enter the chemical recovery loop.

### II. Pyrolytic Fuel Recovery Framework
Pyrolysis breaks down the long-chain hydrocarbon molecules of plastics into shorter-chain liquid and gaseous fuels. The process requires a closed system operating under three phases:

#### A. Thermal Breakdown (The Reactor)
- Feedstock is sealed into an airtight batch reactor chamber.
- The chamber is purged of oxygen (using inert gas or vacuum extraction) to prevent combustion.
- External heat raises the chamber temperature to **350°C–450°C**. At this threshold, carbon-carbon bonds fracture, vaporizing the solid plastic into gaseous hydrocarbons.

#### B. Condensation & Liquid Capture
- The vaporized gases are channeled out of the reactor into a multi-stage condensation array.
- Heavy-to-medium hydrocarbon chains condense out at decreasing temperatures, turning into a liquid pyrolytic oil (synthetic crude).
- This oil represents high-density stored chemical energy, which can be stored, used in modified motor-generators, or used as heating fuel for downstream Forge processes.

#### C. Non-Condensable Syngas Channeling
- Light hydrocarbon gases (such as methane, ethane, and propane) will not condense at ambient room temperatures.
- These remaining gases (Syngas) are pulled continuously from the end of the condensation array to maintain a low-pressure state.
- **Critical Safety Boundary:** This syngas must never be allowed to accumulate in unvented spaces. It must be immediately routed to a designated burner or channeled directly into the input manifolds of the **Air Scrubber** for scrubbing and environmental immobilization.

## Auditor Notes & Unknowns

### PL-001 — Halogenated Polymer Contamination (PVC/Teflon)
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Open |
| Risk | High |
| Priority | Critical |
| Type | Chemical Safety |
| Blocking | Yes (before hot operational runs) |
| Owner | This module / Operations/Air_Scrubber.md |
| First Logged | 2026-05-26 |
| Last Reviewed | 2026-05-26 |

**Description:** PVC and other halogenated plastics release highly corrosive hydrochloric acid (HCl) gas and toxic dioxins when subjected to heat. 
**Why It Matters:** HCl will corrode steel reactor vessels from the inside out and easily bypass basic carbon filtration, creating a severe structural and toxic environmental hazard.
**Resolution Path:** Define a strict testing doctrine at triage (e.g., copper-wire Beilstein test or density sorting) to reject PVC from the pyrolysis loop, and verify that `Air_Scrubber.md` contains an alkaline buffering stage capable of neutralizing accidental acid gas bypass.

### PL-002 — Reactor Thermal Runaway & Pressure Control
| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Status | Open |
| Risk | Medium-High |
| Priority | Major |
| Type | Mechanical Engineering |
| Blocking | Yes |
| Owner | This module |
| First Logged | 2026-05-26 |
| Last Reviewed | 2026-05-26 |

**Description:** The rate of vapor generation must not exceed the throughput capacity of the condensation plumbing.
**Why It Matters:** Rapid gas expansion inside a hot, sealed container can cause sudden over-pressurization, leading to explosive mechanical rupture.
**Resolution Path:** Specify a passive, high-reliability mechanical relief system (like a liquid-sealed bubbler lock) that allows emergency pressure relief without allowing ambient air back into the hot reactor.

**Description:** Recovered synthetic crude oil is often highly acidic and contains wax paraffins that separate and solidify at room temperature.
**Why It Matters:** Unrefined oil may clog secondary engine injectors or rapidly corrode standard fuel tanks if left untreated.
**Resolution Path:** Characterize minimum filtration or post-process washing steps required to render the oil stable for long-term storage in the Forge's auxiliary power reserves.
